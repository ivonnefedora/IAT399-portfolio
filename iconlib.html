Icon Library

It started as a small project of creating several icons which then led me into the idea of creating a user interface library. I created a bank different race of people icons; different body colors, eyes color, hairstyles, clothing, and accessories with front and side view of each. All are exported into png file which can be dragged by users. There are 2 versions, the “ready to be dragged” version and the “mix & match” version. The “ready to be dragged” version is ready to be used for anything containing full body form as well as half body. The “mix & match” version let the users be little bit more creative where they are able to choose different body color, different hairstyles, different facial expression, and different clothing. The purpose of this library is to increase consistency and efficiency throughout media platforms of EAL & WIL team by using same design style throughout.

Challenges

Illustrating Skills: I have never illustrate human figures before and it was a challenge to draw front and side views.

Solution: I learned from illustrators who are expert in drawing human figures

Illustrating in different views: The project requires me to draw front and side view of each human figure. I had trouble with it at first considering it was my first time illustrating a human figure.

Solution: Recalling IAT 106, I learned to draw front, side and isometric view of objects therefore, I apply what was taught to this project using guiding lines and illustrating the front view first.

Reflection

This project has improved my research skill and interaction design skill. I believe, the work my teammate and I have created for this app is valuable because throughout the 3 weeks span of this project, we are able to deeply learn about the app. It was a great chance for us to tackle an uncommon app in our society.
